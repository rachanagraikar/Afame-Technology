Air Quality Prediction Using CNN

This project is part of my Internship (2022), focusing on Artificial Intelligence using PyTorch, conducted at Ekathva Innovation, Shimoga. It leverages the power of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to analyze air quality data and predict pollutant levels effectively.

The primary goal of this project was to develop an AI model capable of predicting air quality metrics like PM2.5, PM10, CO, NO2, and more using historical data and real-time inputs. This system can assist urban planners, environmentalists, and governments in mitigating air pollution and taking proactive measures to improve air quality.

The project involved processing large datasets, feature extraction, and model training using PyTorch, achieving high prediction accuracy. The trained CNN model demonstrates the capability to classify air quality into predefined categories (e.g., Good, Moderate, Poor) based on the pollutants' levels.

Internship Highlights

During this internship, I acquired expertise in PyTorch, CNN architectures, and environmental data modeling. The project emphasized the importance of AI in addressing environmental challenges, paving the way for future innovations in predictive modeling and pollution control.
